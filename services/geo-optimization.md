## What is GEO?

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing digital content to achieve prominent placement in the responses generated by AI-powered search engines and large language models (LLMs) — including ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini, Claude, and emerging platforms.

Unlike traditional SEO, which optimizes for link-based ranking algorithms, GEO optimizes for how AI models understand, contextualize, and cite information when generating natural language responses.

## The Shift from SEO to GEO

| Traditional SEO | Generative Engine Optimization |
| :--- | :--- |
| Optimize for keyword ranking | Optimize for entity understanding |
| Backlinks as trust signals | Citation authority and source diversity |
| Page-level optimization | Topic-level authority modeling |
| Search engine crawlers | AI model training data |
| Click-through rates | Citation frequency and position |

## The GEO Score Framework

Our proprietary GEO Score evaluates content across five critical dimensions:

### 1. **Entity Completeness**
Are your entities (people, places, concepts, products) fully described with structured data? AI models pull from knowledge graphs — if your entities aren't there, you don't exist.

### 2. **Topical Authority**
Do you demonstrate comprehensive coverage of a topic cluster? Models prioritize sources that demonstrate breadth and depth of knowledge on a subject.

### 3. **Citation Readiness**
Is your content formatted so AI models can easily cite it? This includes clear attribution, verifiable claims, and structured references.

### 4. **Semantic Richness**
Does your content use natural language that aligns with how people ask questions? GEO content is conversational, question-aware, and semantically dense.

### 5. **Trust & Credibility**
How authoritative is your content in the eyes of AI models? E-E-A-T signals, source citations, and factual consistency all matter.
